Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Can foreigners buy property on Saba?

Yes! Saba is part of the Caribbean Netherlands, and foreign nationals can purchase property. However, there may be specific requirements regarding residency permits if you plan to live on the island. We can guide you through the process.

How do I get to Saba for property viewings?

Saba is accessible via short flights from St. Maarten (12 minutes) or by ferry (90 minutes). We're happy to help coordinate your visit and arrange property tours during your stay.

What currencies are accepted for real estate transactions?

Property transactions on Saba are typically conducted in US Dollars (USD). The island's official currency is the US Dollar, making transactions straightforward for American and international buyers.

Do you offer virtual property tours?

Absolutely! For clients who can't visit in person, we offer comprehensive virtual tours via video call, including 360° views, neighborhood walks, and Q&A sessions. Contact us to schedule a virtual viewing.

What are the typical closing costs on Saba?

Closing costs on Saba typically range from 6-8% of the purchase price, including transfer tax, notary fees, and registration. We provide a detailed breakdown for each property during the buying process.
